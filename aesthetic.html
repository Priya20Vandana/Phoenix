Types of Fashion Aesthetics: Ditch Trends, Own Your Style

With the recent surge of Instagram and Pinterest back on the popularity track, fashion aesthetics are gaining more attention than ever before. In addition to Y2K fashion resurfacing, Gen Z isn’t holding back on putting forward their sartorial choices while paying homage to nostalgic trends. The internet is bursting with words like cottagecore or dark academia, words that didn’t exist a while ago. So if you are new to the divergent range of fashion aesthetics and aren’t entirely sure of which category you belong you, then this article is guaranteed to bring you some clarity. Or if you are already aware of your individual style but wish to experiment with something different then here are some stunning inspo for you to bookmark and recreate them.



VSCO Girl

The name is derived from the photo-editing app called visco and a typical outfit would feature white sneakers, high-waisted denim jeans or shorts, and oversized t-shirts. The usual hairstyle consists of messy buns with scrunchies and is accessorized up with humble trinkets like seashell necklaces or charms, to support the low-key, effortlessly chill vibe.

Cottagecore

A fashion aesthetic that manifests a dreamy land, surrounded by a garden, living in a small cute cottage by the river while wearing a breezy satin nightgown. Well, cottagecore is exactly this pure bliss. The idea is initiated and supported by young adults or teenagers who romanticize rural life and simple living in nature. You can easily adopt the look by donning a cream flowy midi-dress with hair down and perhaps a bow to go along the humble vibe.

Softgirl Aesthetic

With similar aesthetics as the VSCO, soft girl embracing pastel palettes along with bright cutesy accessories. Your typical soft-girl outfit can include subtle pinks, lavender, and purple hues with pleated skirts, fuzzy cardigans, and sweater vests and florals. The overall goal is to give off a gentle soft aura. Some recent iterations include contrasting silhouettes and patterns like chunky boots, statement accessories, and noir baguette bags.



Grunge

Bringing back the 80s and 90s rock era when bands like Nirvana and Guns N’ Roses dominated and flannel shirts with denim were the staples. The grunge aesthetic embraces numerous dark silhouettes and mixed prints, adopting loads of mesh pieces, heavy combat boots, and chokers, especially layered ones. Perhaps adding a guitar in the background while clicking photos in one of those vintage film reel cameras will further add to this equation, and if the camera isn’t available then polaroid can always do the trick.



Streetwear

Streetwear fashion has gained fresh popularity recently, making it the go-to option for many off-duty looks of supermodels and singers. A classic streetwear ensemble would consist of a graphic tee, baggy jeans, and jordans. You can always do a little mix and match with different textures like styling your favourite knit sweatpants with a varsity or puffer jacket to add a dash of coolness to the outfit and don’t forget to accessorize up with chunky jewellery.

E-girl

Deriving its features from anime aesthetics, an e-girl (electronic girl) is typical of someone who is well associated with the internet and invests their majority of time online. The usual features could involve dyed hair, often concentrated on the front sections in vibrant neon hues. Overdramatized makeup to exaggerate and highlight their eyes and cheeks. Cutesy drawings like tiny hearts or freckles, extra blush, and accessories including hair clips, chokers, and mainly silver jewellery.



Dark Academia

The dark academia aesthetic draws inspiration from Greek art, literature, and writing while also infusing Gothic elements. The fashion also emphasizes education and has a vintage preppy vibe to it. Recreating looks of old English private schools and encompassing designs and silhouettes like crewneck sweaters, tweed pants, fine cardigans, and button-down oxfords. The overall aura is more autumn-esque with a darker palette consisting of browns, beige, chestnut, and more.

Light Academia

Light academia has the same background as dark academia, the only slight difference lies in the colour palette. Light academia aesthetic consists of hues and shades like cream, whites, soft beige, and so on. To look the part simply invest in relaxed and cosy silhouettes while going for fabrics like cashmere, cotton, linens, and corduroy. And stay true to neutral notes.




Monochromatic

While monochromatic ensembles have repeatedly been associated with bland, Gen Z and current influencers are showing the new side of monochromatic outfits to the world. While incorporating various textures and silhouettes you can easily rock the single colour look. And if you aren’t that big on enthusiasm or styling pieces together then simply don a co-ord set with similar hue footwear and accessories.
